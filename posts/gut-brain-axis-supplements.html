The gut produces approximately 90% of the body's serotonin and hosts the enteric nervous system — sometimes called the "second brain." The gut-brain axis is a bidirectional communication network, meaning chronic gut dysfunction directly impacts cognitive function and mood. Here are the supplements with the most credible evidence for supporting this system.

Chronic stress is one of the most damaging factors for gut barrier integrity — cortisol increases intestinal permeability and disrupts the microbiome composition within days. KSM-66 is the most studied ashwagandha extract, with multiple RCTs showing significant reductions in cortisol levels and perceived stress. Targeting the stress axis is therefore one of the most direct ways to support gut health in chronically stressed individuals. KSM-66 specifically — not generic ashwagandha root powder.

L-Glutamine Powder (Pharmaceutical Grade)

Amino Acid
FormFree-Form
Purity99%+
Dose5g daily

Glutamine is the primary fuel source for enterocytes — the cells lining the intestinal wall. During periods of gut stress, illness, or intense exercise, glutamine becomes conditionally essential: the body's demand exceeds what can be synthesised. Supplemental glutamine at 5g daily has evidence for supporting intestinal permeability markers and is widely used in clinical nutrition for gut recovery. Buy unflavoured pharmaceutical-grade powder — not the flavoured sports versions which contain unnecessary additives.

Magnesium is required for over 300 enzymatic processes in the body and is deficient in an estimated 70% of UK adults. Its relevance for the gut-brain axis is threefold: it supports the enteric nervous system directly, regulates the stress response via the HPA axis, and has evidence for improving sleep quality — and poor sleep is one of the fastest ways to disrupt the microbiome. Glycinate is the form with the highest bioavailability and the least laxative effect at therapeutic doses. Avoid magnesium oxide — it's poorly absorbed and primarily used as a laxative.
