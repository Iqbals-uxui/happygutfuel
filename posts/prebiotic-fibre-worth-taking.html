Fibermaxxing became a genuine trend in 2025 for good reason — most people in the UK consume less than half the recommended daily fibre intake. But not all fibre feeds your microbiome equally. Prebiotic fibres are a specific subset that selectively feed beneficial bacteria. Here's what actually has evidence behind it.

Inulin is the most researched prebiotic fibre type and the one with the broadest evidence base for increasing Bifidobacterium populations. Chicory-derived inulin at 95%+ FOS purity is the most bioavailable form. The caveat is its fast fermentation rate — starting too high too quickly causes significant gas and bloating. Start at 3g daily and increase slowly over several weeks. Not recommended as a first prebiotic if you have a history of IBS.

Unmodified Potato Starch (Resistant Starch RS2)

Resistant Starch
TypeRS2
SourceRaw Potato
Starting Dose1 tbsp daily

Resistant starch is the most underrated prebiotic — and unmodified potato starch is one of the cheapest and most concentrated sources available. RS2 starch resists digestion in the small intestine entirely, arriving in the colon intact where it preferentially feeds Butyrivibrio and other butyrate-producing species. Butyrate is the primary fuel source for colonocytes — the cells lining your gut wall. Start with a teaspoon in cold water and build up slowly.

Pros
  • Feeds butyrate-producing bacteria — key for gut wall health
  • Exceptional value — one of the lowest cost prebiotics per gram
  • Feeds different bacterial populations than inulin or acacia
Cons
  • Must be consumed cold — heat converts RS2 to digestible starch
  • Chalky texture in water — mixes better into smoothies or yoghurt
